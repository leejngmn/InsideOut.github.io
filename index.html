What if 3D objects could show both surface details and hidden structures simultaneously?
We explore two distinct imaging modalities that capture complementary aspects of the world: RGB imaging reveals rich surface textures and colors, while X-ray imaging penetrates through materials to expose internal structures. These modalities are naturally complementary but have never been unified in 3D reconstruction. InsideOut bridges this gap by extending 3D Gaussian Splatting to fuse RGB and X-ray data, enabling unprecedented visualization capabilities for medical diagnostics, cultural heritage analysis, and manufacturing inspection.

Abstract

We introduce InsideOut, an extension of 3D Gaussian splatting (3DGS) that bridges the gap between high-fidelity RGB surface details and subsurface X-ray structures. The fusion of RGB and X-ray imaging is invaluable in fields such as medical diagnostics, cultural heritage restoration, and manufacturing. We collect new paired RGB and X-ray data, perform hierarchical fitting to align RGB and X-ray radiative Gaussian splats, and propose an X-ray reference loss to ensure consistent internal structures. InsideOut effectively addresses the challenges posed by disparate data representations between the two modalities and limited paired datasets. This approach significantly extends the applicability of 3DGS, enhancing visualization, simulation, and non-destructive testing capabilities across various domains.

Framework Overview

InsideOut's framework is a five-stage pipeline for integrating RGB and X-ray images into a unified 3DGS model that captures both external appearance and internal structures.


1. Point Cloud Initialization: Generate initial point clouds for RGB Gaussians using Structure from Motion (SfM) and for radiative (X-ray) Gaussians using Angle Cuboid Uniform Initialization (ACUI).

2. Surface Rendering: Train RGB Gaussians with standard 3DGS to learn external surfaces and radiative Gaussians with X-Gaussian to capture coarse internal structures.

3. Hierarchical Fitting: Align the two modalities geometrically through global, local, and fine fitting stages to match positions, scales, and rotations.

4. Internal Rendering: Refine structures using cross-sectional X-ray slices as guidance, applying an X-ray reference loss to sharpen boundaries and reduce noise.

5. Visual Alignment: Transfer colors from RGB to radiative Gaussians and clone fine-detail RGB splats (bottom 95% by covariance) to enhance surface fidelity without disrupting internals.


Qualitative Results

Internal Rendering

InsideOut eliminates internal noise and scattered Gaussian splats, focusing them along the object's surface for a cleaner representation. It also enhances the visibility of internal layers, producing sharper and more well-defined boundaries.

Mesh Reconstruction

InsideOut maintains the overall shape integrity of both external and internal structures by integrating volumetric information with surface details. InsideOut captures diverse normal distributions, indicating richer geometric complexity compared to methods that rely on single modalities.


Application

InsideOut simultaneously visualizes external surface appearance and internal structural details, facilitating comprehensive analysis and physics-based simulation.


Real-world Asset Diagnosis

InsideOut delivers real-world 3D assets that support quantitative volume measurements and cross-sectional analysis, moving beyond mere visual inspection to evidence-based assessment.


Interactive Asset

Interactive assets enable virtual exploration and manipulation, allowing users to reveal internal structures through dynamic interaction while preserving physical specimens.
